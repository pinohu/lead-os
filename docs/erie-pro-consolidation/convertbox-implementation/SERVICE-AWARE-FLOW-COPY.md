# Erie.Pro Service-Aware ConvertBox Flow Copy

Date: 2026-05-10

This replaces the generic six-step wording with service-aware visitor copy. The current ConvertBox drafts should be treated as mechanical shells that prove steps, targeting, forms, and styling work. They are not final customer experience until these family-specific questions and messages are applied.

## Core Rule

Do not ask every visitor the same questions.

ConvertBox should infer context from the URL/service page, then ask only the next useful question for that service type. A plumbing visitor needs leak, drain, water heater, and shutoff questions. A mental health visitor needs private appointment language. A funeral home visitor needs gentle, non-salesy help. A snow removal visitor needs storm timing and property type.

## Step Pattern

Each service family still uses six native ConvertBox steps, but the names and copy must adapt to the family:

1. Context step: confirm the service-specific need.
2. Triage step: ask the highest-signal service question.
3. Details step: collect the minimum useful job or appointment details.
4. Timing step: urgency, date, or preferred appointment window.
5. Contact step: name, phone, email, consent.
6. Confirmation step: reassure the visitor about the next step.

## Family Copy Packs

### Emergency Home Response

Services:
Plumbing, HVAC, Electrical, Roofing, Garage Door, Appliance Repair, Septic & Sewer, Water Damage Restoration, Fire Damage Restoration, Storm Damage Repair, Water Heater Services, Drain Cleaning, Sewer Line Repair, AC Repair, Furnace Repair, Ice Dam Removal, Emergency Board-Up, Basement Flood Cleanup, Locksmith Services.

Visitor psychology:
The visitor may be stressed, interrupted, worried about property damage, or trying to protect family safety. Speed and reassurance matter more than explanation.

Opening copy:
`Need urgent help in Erie County? Tell us what is happening and we will help route your request to the right local pro.`

Service-specific triage questions:
- Plumbing: `Is water actively leaking, backing up, or shut off?`
- HVAC: `Is this no heat, no cooling, poor airflow, or maintenance?`
- Electrical: `Do you see sparks, smell burning, have exposed wiring, or have a power outage?`
- Roofing/storm: `Is there an active leak, missing shingles, tree damage, or insurance claim?`
- Garage door: `Is the door stuck open, stuck closed, off track, or spring/cable related?`
- Septic/sewer/drain: `Is sewage backing up, draining slowly, or is this an inspection/pumping request?`
- Restoration: `Is water, smoke, fire, mold, or storm damage active right now?`
- Locksmith: `Are you locked out now, rekeying, installing, or repairing a lock?`

Field set:
Phone, service issue, safety/damage flag, property type, Erie County community or zip, short description, preferred contact method.

Confirmation copy:
`Thanks. We have the urgent details and will route this through the Erie County service path that fits the issue. Keep your phone nearby.`

## URL Targeting Rule

For service pages, the ConvertBox should use the page slug as context:

- `/plumbing`, `/drain-cleaning`, `/sewer-line-repair`, and `/water-heater-services` should not open with generic `Tell us what you need`; they should open with plumbing-specific options.
- `/mental-health-counseling` should never show contractor language or urgency pressure.
- `/funeral-homes` should never show promotional language like `Get matched free` as the emotional lead.
- `/snow-removal`, `/salt-deicing-services`, and `/commercial-snow-removal` should emphasize Erie winter timing and property safety.
- `/boat-repair-marine-services`, `/dock-installation-repair`, and `/marina-boat-winterization` should reference marine/location/seasonal readiness.
