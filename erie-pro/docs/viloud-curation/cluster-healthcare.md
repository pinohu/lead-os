For Phase 1, single channel is fine. Programming should rotate across audience types.

## Healthcare-specific filter

Critical: **avoid medical misinformation.** Many YouTube healthcare channels are non-credentialed or fringe. Vet EVERY video by:

- Creator credentials visible (MD, DDS, DC, OD, RVN, LCSW, AuD, etc.)
- Content cites peer-reviewed sources
- Tone is educational, not sales
- Not selling supplements or alternative-medicine products

Skip:

- "Healing crystals" content
- Anti-vaccine content
- Diet/wellness influencer content from non-credentialed creators
- Aggressive cures-from-home content
- Anti-conventional-medicine content

**This cluster has the highest legal/reputational risk if curation is sloppy.** Take more time vetting healthcare videos than any other cluster.

## Sub-niche specializations

- **`dental`** / **`dentist`** — biggest sub-niche by Erie demand. Vet child-dentistry content carefully (different audience)
- **`chiropractic`** — controversial in some medical circles. Stick to content that's evidence-based and avoids exaggerated claims
- **`veterinary`** — entirely separate audience (pet owners). Strong argument for dedicated channel from day one
- **`dermatology`** — high search volume; visual content (rashes, moles) needs warning frames
- **`optometry`** — relatively small content pool; supplement with major brand content (Warby Parker, etc.)
- **`physical-therapy`** — Bob & Brad alone could fill this niche; one of the strongest single-creator pools in healthcare
- **`mental-health-counseling`** — TONE-CRITICAL. Visitors often in crisis. Therapy in a Nutshell is the safest source
- **`senior-home-care`** / **`home-health-care`** — entirely different audience (adult children of aging parents). Worth dedicated channel in Phase 2
- **`hearing-aids-audiology`** — small content pool; pull manufacturer education (Phonak, Oticon, Starkey)

Phase 2 split priorities:
1. **`veterinary`** → own channel (different audience)
2. **`senior-home-care`** + **`home-health-care`** → own channel (different audience)
3. **`mental-health-counseling`** → own channel (sensitive audience, distinct tone needs)
