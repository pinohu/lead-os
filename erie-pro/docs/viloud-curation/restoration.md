## Erie-specific filter

Restoration content for Erie should emphasize:

- **Basement flooding** — most Erie homes have basements, lake-effect rain events are common
- **Ice dam interior damage** — distinctly Erie/snow-belt
- **Sump pump failures** — a major Erie home-emergency category
- **Freeze-thaw foundation cracks** — leads to seepage and mold
- **Storm wind damage** — Lake Erie wind events are real

Skip videos that:

- Focus exclusively on hurricane-zone damage (irrelevant for Erie audience)
- Use dramatic music or sensationalized framing (wrong tone for distressed visitors)
- Promote single national-chain restoration companies aggressively (we don't want to send Erie traffic to non-local competitors)

## Channel branding

- **Name:** `Erie Restoration TV`
- **Description:** `Calm, educational content on water damage, mold, fire, and storm restoration for Erie homeowners.`
- **Tone:** Reassuring. Avoid emergency-red color schemes; use neutral blues/greens.
- **Schedule:** Linear loop

## Important content note

For this niche specifically, watch for **"storm chaser" / disaster-tourism content**. Some restoration channels exist primarily to scare homeowners into expensive contracts. Vet videos for educational value vs. fear-marketing — the ratio should be heavy on education.

If a video's thumbnail uses red explosion graphics, "SHOCKING" text, or emotionally manipulative framing, skip it. The Erie homeowner who lands on this page during an active crisis needs reassurance and competence-signal, not more anxiety.
