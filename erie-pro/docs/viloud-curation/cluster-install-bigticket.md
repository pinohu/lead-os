## Niches covered

- `solar` (anchor)
- `pool-spa` / `spa`

These niches share a profile: **high-research-intent visitors, long sales cycles, $5k-$50k purchase decisions.** Content should be longer-form and decision-oriented than the other clusters.

## Erie-specific filter

This cluster has the strongest climate-dependent variation:

- **Solar in Erie** — works, but payback period is longer (less sun than Arizona). PA has reasonable solar incentives. SREC market is changing
- **Pools in Erie** — short season (Jun-Sep), heated pools extend it. Many Erie homes can't justify cost — but those that do should see real Erie-specific content
- **Home security** — climate-agnostic
- **Insulation** — HUGE for Erie. Heating bills are 40-50% of utility costs. Insulation ROI is among the best home improvements in the region. Prioritize this content heavily

Skip:

- Solar content from Arizona/Texas (different economics)
- Pool content from year-round-warm climates
- Insulation content from warm climates (different R-value requirements)

## Sub-niche specializations

- **`solar`** — high-intent, low-volume niche. Visitors here are 6-12 months from purchase. Content should reduce anxiety and provide credentialing signal
- **`pool-spa`** — split or merge with hot-tub-specific content if directory gets distinct lead types
- **`home-security`** / **`alarm`** — increasingly DIY-able (Ring, SimpliSafe). Vet content to balance pro-install vs. DIY perspectives without alienating either
- **`insulation`** — probably the strongest "Erie homeowner pays back fast" content in the entire directory. Consider dedicated channel in Phase 2 with heavy local emphasis (Erie average heating bill, R-value upgrade payback math)
