**Channel-splitting recommendation:** This cluster is genuinely too diverse for a single channel — pest control and photography share no audience. **Strong recommendation: split into 4 sub-channels in Phase 2**:

1. **Erie Pest & Wildlife TV** — pest-control, bat-removal, bee-wasp-removal, wildlife-removal
2. **Erie Marine TV** — boat-repair, dock-install, marina (lake-effect appropriate, distinctive)
3. **Erie Junk & Demolition TV** — junk-removal, dumpster-rental, demolition, excavation
4. **Erie Construction TV** — general-contractor, home-builders, home-remodeling
5. **Erie Pets, Photography, Moving** — too small to split further; keep in catch-all

Phase 1 catch-all "Erie Niche Services TV" with single channel is acceptable if you want to minimize setup time.

## Erie-specific filter

Several sub-niches have strong Erie relevance:

- **Marine** — Lake Erie is THE differentiator. Erie marina/dock/boat content is meaningful here while irrelevant in landlocked areas
- **Wildlife** — Erie has urban wildlife (raccoons, squirrels in older neighborhoods, occasional black bear in outer areas)
- **Snow-load demolition** — Erie buildings damaged by snow loads have specific demolition needs
- **Pest seasonality** — Erie pests peak May-Sept; quiet season Dec-Feb. Programming can lean seasonal

Skip:

- Subtropical pest content (palm-tree rats, cockroach-specific Florida content)
- Hurricane-debris content (tornadoes occur in PA but hurricane-specific construction doesn't apply)
- Pelagic / saltwater marine content (Erie is freshwater)

## Sub-niche specializations (Phase 2 channel priorities)

When splitting in Phase 2, prioritize in this order based on audience uniqueness:

1. **Marine cluster** (boat-repair, dock-install, marina) — most distinctive audience, Erie-specific
2. **Pest & wildlife** — clear audience, recurring need
3. **Construction** (general-contractor, builders, remodel) — high-value transactions
4. **Junk & demolition** — different from construction; one-off transactional

The catch-all niches (photography, moving, pet-grooming) can stay shared until they generate enough lead volume to justify dedicated channels.
