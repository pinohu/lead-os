## Erie-specific filter

Erie outdoor content needs to handle real winter:

- **Snow events 30-50× per year** — snow-removal content needs to address Erie-scale snowfall, not Atlanta-scale flurries
- **Lake-effect timing** — Erie snows when surrounding areas don't (winds off Lake Erie can dump 6+ inches in hours)
- **60-80 freeze-thaw cycles** — affects pavement, hardscape, asphalt, foundations
- **Spring mud season is brutal** — many southern-US lawn-care channels gloss over this
- **Tree species** are northern hardwood — most southern tree-removal channels focus on pines, palms, etc.
- **Lakefront properties** have unique drainage, erosion, and zoning concerns

Skip videos that:

- Focus on warm-climate landscaping (drought-tolerant Southwest, etc.)
- Cover only southern grass species (St. Augustine, Bermuda — Erie is cool-season grass country)
- Use light-snow content from mid-Atlantic regions as "winter" reference

## Sub-niche specializations (notes for the curator)

Most sub-niches share the same channel content but some have distinct nuances:

- **`tree-service` / `tree-removal`** — emphasize safety, large-equipment content, ISA certification topics
- **`gutters`** — heavy autumn-leaves focus; Erie's tree-density creates more gutter work than national average
- **`irrigation`** — winterization is mandatory in Erie; year-round irrigation content from southern US is misleading
- **`outdoor-lighting` vs. `holiday-lighting`** — outdoor is permanent (landscape, security); holiday is seasonal install/uninstall. Different vendors typically.
- **`lakefront-property-maintenance`** — niche; emphasize erosion control, dock care, ice-pressure-on-walls concerns
- **`asphalt-sealing` / `driveway-paving`** — different from concrete-driveway content (which goes in trades-exterior cluster)

If any of these niches need their own dedicated channel based on Phase 1 data, prioritize: tree-service (different audience risk profile) and lakefront-property-maintenance (very different audience).
